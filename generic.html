Quadruped Guide Dog Robot

I led a team to design a power-efficient, lightweight quadruped guide dog robot for visually impaired individuals. This project addresses the limitations of existing robots like the Unitree Go 1. The robot is designed to fit in a small carry on while still being able to traverse 20x20 cm staircase pretty smoothly. I contributed to multiple aspects of the project, including designing the 3D printed body and securing the electronics in place. Additionally, I worked on various analyses such as structural, kinematics, dynamics, and stair-climbing analysis.

Strong, Accurate, and Low-Cost Robotic Arm (Published @ Humanoids 2025)

This is Forte. A final year honors thesis turned into a paper accepted in Humanoids 2025, an international robotics conference held in Seoul, South Korea.

Forte is a fully 3D-printable, 6-DoF robotic arm designed to deliver near industrial-grade performance—0.63 kg payload, 0.467 m reach, and sub-millimeter repeatability—at a material cost under $215.. Its design combines capstan-based cable drives, timing belts, and topology-optimized 3D-printed structures to maximize stiffness and minimize backlash while keeping components affordable and accessible. Unlike most low-cost educational arms, Forte achieves high precision and strength without relying on expensive electronics or manufacturing, making it an effective platform for both classroom education and advanced robotics research. The system is powered by readily available microcontrollers and open-source control software, providing a complete hardware–software stack for experimentation and extension.

Humonnaoid From the Torso Up

I am leading the robotics mechanical team to prepare for the MassRobotics competition 2025. We are desigining, fabricating, and testing a humanoid from the torso Up that serves as a bartender. The Humanoid includes a torso, two 7 Degrees-of-Freedom (DOF) robotic arms, and a 3 DOF neck. I divided the team into subteams to work on subsystems (shoulder, elbow, and wrist) and be more efficient. The shoulder contains two DOFs, one being a capstan mechanism. Elbow is two DOFs (Pitch and Roll). Pitch motion is run through a cable transmission mechancism. Roll motion includes a planetary gear. The wrist features 3 DOFs including a gripper. Neo rev 21, AK60-6 motors as well as STS3215 servos were used all over the robot to provide sufficient torque to lift up a 750 ml wine bottle.
